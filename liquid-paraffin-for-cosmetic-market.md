<p><p>Liquid paraffin, commonly used in cosmetics, is categorized into two main types: paraffin-based and cycloalkyl-based. Paraffin-based liquid paraffin is derived from petroleum and serves as an effective moisturizer, providing a protective barrier on the skin. In contrast, cycloalkyl-based liquid paraffin, which includes compounds like cyclopentasiloxane, offers a lighter, non-greasy feel while enhancing spreadability in formulations. Both types are valued for their emollient properties, contributing to skin hydration and improving the texture of cosmetic products.</p></p>
