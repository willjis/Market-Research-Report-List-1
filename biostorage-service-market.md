<p><p>The biostorage service market comprises several types, each tailored to specific preservation needs. Ambient storage involves maintaining samples at room temperature, suitable for stable materials. Refrigerated storage keeps samples at lower temperatures to maintain biological integrity, ideal for short-term storage. Frozen storage utilizes sub-zero temperatures for long-term preservation of sensitive biomaterials. Ultra-low storage further reduces temperatures to extreme lows, preserving vital samples for extended periods. Other storage methods include specialty environments designed for unique sample requirements.</p></p>
