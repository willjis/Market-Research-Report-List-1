<p><p>Real-Time Clocks (RTC) are essential components in electronic devices, maintaining accurate timekeeping even when powered off. The RTC market is divided into two main types: soft clocks and hard clocks. Soft clocks rely on software to maintain time, often using microcontroller functionalities, making them flexible but dependent on power. In contrast, hard clocks are standalone hardware solutions that utilize battery backup for continuous timekeeping, ensuring accuracy during power outages. Both types cater to diverse application requirements, balancing cost and reliability.</p></p>
