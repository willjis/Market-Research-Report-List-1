<p><p>The punctal occluder market is divided into two main types: non-dissolvable and dissolvable occluders. Non-dissolvable occluders are made from materials that do not break down over time, providing a permanent solution for conditions like dry eye by blocking tear drainage. In contrast, dissolvable occluders are designed to biodegrade after a certain period, offering a temporary option for patients needing short-term relief without long-lasting alterations in tear flow. Each type caters to specific patient needs and treatment durations.</p></p>
