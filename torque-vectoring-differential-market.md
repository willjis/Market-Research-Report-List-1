<p><ul><li>Active Torque Vectoring Differential</li><li>Passive Torque Vectoring Differential</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The torque vectoring differential market is divided into two main types: active and passive torque vectoring differentials. Active torque vectoring differentials use electronic controls and sensors to dynamically distribute power between wheels, enhancing traction and handling during various driving conditions. Conversely, passive torque vectoring differentials rely on mechanical systems such as gears and clutches to distribute torque, offering improved performance without electronic intervention. Both types aim to optimize vehicle stability, cornering, and overall driving experience, catering to different automotive segments.</p></p>
