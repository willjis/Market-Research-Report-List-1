<p><p>High-performance pigments (HPPs) are specialized colorants known for their superior durability, brightness, and stability. The market for HPPs is generally divided into two main types: organic HPPs and inorganic HPPs. Organic HPPs are derived from carbon-based compounds, offering vibrant colors and excellent tinting strength, making them popular in coatings and plastics. In contrast, inorganic HPPs, typically mineral-based, provide exceptional chemical resistance and UV stability, making them suitable for applications in architecture and automotive industries. Both types cater to distinct industrial needs.</p></p>
