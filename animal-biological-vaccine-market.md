<p><p>The animal biological vaccine market is categorized into several administration routes: oral, subcutaneous, intramuscular, and others. Oral vaccines are administered through the mouth, facilitating easy dosing for livestock. Subcutaneous vaccines are injected beneath the skin, offering localized immune response. Intramuscular vaccines are injected directly into muscle tissue, ensuring rapid absorption into the bloodstream. Other routes may include ocular or intranasal administration, catering to specific animal needs. Each type plays a crucial role in enhancing animal health and disease prevention.</p></p>
