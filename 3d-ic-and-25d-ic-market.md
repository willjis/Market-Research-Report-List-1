<p><p>3D IC (Three-Dimensional Integrated Circuit) and 2.5D IC (Two-and-a-Half Dimensional Integrated Circuit) are advanced semiconductor packaging technologies designed to enhance performance, reduce power consumption, and improve space efficiency. In a 3D IC, multiple layers of silicon wafers or chips are stacked vertically, allowing for shorter interconnects and greater integration of functionalities. On the other hand, 2.5D IC uses a shared interposer that connects multiple chips side by side on a single substrate, optimizing space and enabling high-bandwidth data transfer.</p><p>The market for 3D and 2.5D ICs is witnessing robust growth, fueled by increasing demand for high-performance computing, mobile applications, and artificial intelligence.
