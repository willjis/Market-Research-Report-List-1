<p><p>The plant-based food colors market comprises several key types, including carotenoids, anthocyanins, chlorophyll, paprika extract, and spirulina extract. Carotenoids, found in carrots and sweet potatoes, provide orange and yellow hues. Anthocyanins, derived from berries, impart red and purple shades. Chlorophyll, sourced from green plants, offers green coloring. Paprika extract, made from ground peppers, delivers vibrant reds and oranges, while spirulina extract, a blue-green algae, adds a unique blue tint. These natural colors cater to growing consumer demand for healthier, plant-based products.</p></p>
