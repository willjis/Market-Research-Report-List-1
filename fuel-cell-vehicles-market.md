<p><ul><li>Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cell</li><li>Alkaline Fuel cell</li><li>Solid Oxide Fuel Cell</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Fuel cell vehicles (FCVs) utilize different types of fuel cells to convert hydrogen into electricity. **Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFC)** are lightweight, efficient, and ideal for passenger vehicles due to their quick startup and compact design. **Alkaline Fuel Cells (AFC)** are highly efficient but less common in vehicles due to sensitivity to impurities. **Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC)** operate at high temperatures, offering high efficiency and durability, making them suitable for heavy-duty applications like trucks and buses, though slower to start. Each type caters to specific market needs.</p></p>
