<p><p>The geotechnical sensors market encompasses various types, each serving specific purposes in monitoring soil and environmental conditions. Extensometers measure ground deformation, crucial for assessing stability in structures. Piezometers monitor groundwater levels and pressure, aiding in flood and slope stability analysis. Rain gauges collect precipitation data, essential for hydrological studies. Thermometers track temperature changes in soil, influencing material behavior. Other sensors include inclinometer and strain gauge technologies, contributing to a comprehensive understanding of geotechnical environments and enhancing safety and engineering practices.</p></p>
