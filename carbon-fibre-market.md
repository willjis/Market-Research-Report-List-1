<p><p>The carbon fiber market is divided into composite and non-composite segments. In the composite market, carbon fibers are combined with resins or polymers to create lightweight, high-strength materials used in aerospace, automotive, and wind energy industries. The non-composite market involves standalone carbon fibers or fabrics used in applications like textiles, filtration, and electronics, where their inherent properties, such as conductivity and heat resistance, are utilized without being embedded in a matrix. Both markets cater to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
