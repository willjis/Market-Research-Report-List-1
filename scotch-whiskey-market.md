<p><p>The Scotch whiskey market is primarily categorized into three main types: Pure Malt Whisky, Grain Whisky, and Blended Whisky. Pure Malt Whisky is made entirely from malted barley in pot stills, offering rich flavors. Grain Whisky, produced from grains other than barley, is typically lighter and smoother, often made in column stills. Blended Whisky combines both malt and grain whiskies, creating a balanced flavor profile. These distinctions cater to diverse consumer preferences, shaping the Scotch whiskey landscape.</p></p>
